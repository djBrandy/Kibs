Our standard 24-marker DNA test involves comparison of 24 different genetic markers in the DNA of the child, father, and mother. The DNA test is based on the fact that 50% of a childâ€™s DNA is inherited from the father and 50% from the mother. At 23 of the 24 locations, a person has 2 numbers each one derived from either parent. This DNA test provides maximum statistical power and is superior to the DNA tests commonly used by most law enforcement labs in the entire Africa.

A paternity index (PI) score of 1 is assigned to each marker that matches the alleged fatherâ€™s and the childâ€™s DNA profile while a PI score of 0 is assigned to every non-match. The PI is the likelihood ratio that indicates whether the evidence fits better with the hypothesis that the man is the father or with the hypothesis that someone else is the father.

The combined paternity index (CPI) is then computed by multiplying the PIs then converted to a probability of paternity value. The larger the CPI, the more strongly the hypothesis that the alleged father is the true biological father is supported by the genetic evidence. The alleged father is ruled out as the biological father if he does not share a number in common at 3 or more genetic systems. Prenatal DNA Paternity Test for Unborn Babies

The prenatal test determines whether a particular man is the biological father of an unborn child. The prenatal paternity test is done before a baby is born to determine whether a particular man is the real biological father of the unborn baby. There are two types of prenatal paternity test namely:

  • The non-invasive prenatal paternity test (NIPPT)

Testing when the Alleged Father is Absent

Paternity can be determined even if the alleged father is unavailable due to death, disappearance or physical separation. In place of the unavailable man, testing may be done using the following samples:

  • Paternal Grandparent (biological mother or known biological father of the man)
  • Paternal uncle or aunt (biological brother or sister of the missing man, born by the same mother)
  • Sibling (biological brother or sister of the child, known to have been fathered by the missing man)
  • Any male relative of the missing man for male children
